Project Overview

This ongoing project investigates the dynamics and underlying causes of mortgage defaults leading up to the 2008 financial crisis. Utilizing a robust dataset that includes mortgage originations, national mortgage statistics, and economic indicators from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) and Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA), the project aims to uncover patterns and correlations that might explain the crisis.

Objectives

The main objectives of this analysis are to evaluate the impact of various financial factors such as interest rates, property values, and borrower creditworthiness on mortgage defaults. The project seeks to understand how these elements interacted to precipitate the financial crisis, with a particular focus on the years leading up to 2008.

Methods

Using R for data manipulation and analysis, the project involves extensive data cleaning, merging different data sources, and conducting statistical analyses. Techniques include correlation analysis, regression models, and visual data explorations to identify trends and outliers.

Status

The project is currently in progress, with preliminary analyses completed on key economic indicators. Ongoing work involves refining data integration and expanding the analysis to include more nuanced aspects of mortgage data.

Initial Findings

Early findings indicate significant correlations between mortgage rates and default rates, as well as the influence of macroeconomic factors like GDP and unemployment rates on mortgage defaults. Detailed results and comprehensive insights will be presented upon project completion.
